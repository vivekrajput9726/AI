Abstract

Synora Health is an AI-powered full-stack healthcare platform designed to revolutionize access to medical services through intelligent technology. The platform enables patients to consult top doctors, analyze symptoms with AI, manage health records, and receive personalized healthcare insights — anytime, anywhere.


The system integrates Explainable AI (XAI) methods including SHAP (Shapley Additive exPlanations) and LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) to provide transparent AI-driven health analysis. The platform uses OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini for natural language symptom analysis, report interpretation, and 24/7 AI health assistance.


Key features include: multi-role authentication (Patient/Doctor/Admin), intelligent appointment booking (Video/Voice/In-Person/Email), AI symptom checker with SHAP explainability, laboratory report analyzer using computer vision, digital medical history with trend charts, emergency SOS with GPS location sharing, mental health assessment (PHQ-9, GAD-7, PSS-10), drug interaction checker, family health manager, vaccination tracker, symptom diary, BMI calculator, and a real-time admin analytics dashboard with six visualization charts.


The backend is developed using FastAPI (Python) with MongoDB as the NoSQL database, and the frontend uses React.js 18 with Tailwind CSS. The platform demonstrates the practical implementation of Artificial Intelligence in primary healthcare, offering fast preliminary diagnosis, patient awareness, and clinical decision support.


Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Healthcare, FastAPI, React.js, MongoDB, GPT-4o-mini, SHAP, LIME, Explainable AI, Telemedicine, Electronic Health Records, Jitsi Meet, Twilio, OpenAI Vision API

1. Introduction

1.1 Introduction

Synora Health is a comprehensive, AI-powered healthcare platform developed to democratize access to quality medical services. The platform serves as a full-stack digital health ecosystem connecting patients, doctors, and administrators through an intelligent, transparent, and accessible interface.

The system leverages cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence technologies including OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini for symptom analysis, SHAP and LIME for explainability, and computer vision for medical report analysis. The driving mission is to use machine learning, explainable AI, and digital health infrastructure to provide fast, transparent, and accessible healthcare services to everyone — regardless of location.

1.2 Background of the Project

This project was developed in response to the documented healthcare access crisis in developing regions. In South Asia and other developing countries, the ratio of doctors to patients remains critically low, resulting in long waiting times, delayed diagnoses, and preventable deterioration of treatable conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and anemia.

Standard diagnostic procedures involve numerous in-person visits, laboratory tests, and specialist referrals — making healthcare both costly and inaccessible to rural and semi-urban populations. As AI technologies have advanced, the feasibility of deploying AI-assisted diagnostic tools at scale has become increasingly practical.

Synora Health was envisioned as a clinically-informed, explainable, and user-friendly platform that accepts standard patient symptoms, provides AI-generated preliminary assessments, connects patients with appropriate specialists, and maintains comprehensive digital health records — all accessible via a standard web browser.

1.3 Problem Statement

Despite tremendous advances in medical science, a significant percentage of patients in underserved areas lack timely access to diagnostic services. Three interconnected problems were identified:

  1. Doctor Shortage: The extreme lack of qualified medical professionals compared to population size causes hospital overloading and long consultation queues, particularly in tier-2 and tier-3 cities.
  2. Late Detection: Patients often present at health facilities with advanced-stage diseases that could have been detected and controlled at earlier stages through simple vital sign monitoring and AI-assisted analysis.
  3. Lack of Transparency: Diagnoses are often delivered without explanation — patients receive verdicts without understanding the contributing factors, reducing treatment adherence and patient engagement.

Existing digital health technologies are either too simplistic (general symptom checkers with no clinical background) or too complex (requiring specialized equipment and professional technical skills). Synora Health was created to fill this gap with an accessible, explainable, AI-powered solution.

1.4 Importance of the System

Synora Health serves as a first-line screening layer in the healthcare continuum. The system bridges the critical time gap between symptom onset and medical consultation by enabling patients to:

  • Access AI-powered symptom analysis instantly without hospital visits
  • Receive transparent, explainable health assessments with SHAP/LIME insights
  • Connect with verified specialist doctors based on AI recommendations
  • Book consultations via multiple channels (Video / Voice / In-Person / Email)
  • Manage complete digital health records with trend analysis
  • Use emergency SOS for critical situations with GPS-based notifications
  • Track mental health, vaccination history, BMI, and symptom patterns

The platform's use of SHAP and LIME explainability, unlike opaque diagnostic systems, allows patients and doctors to see which specific symptoms or report values contributed most to a prediction — building trust and facilitating informed clinical decision-making.

4. Feasibility Study

4.1 Technical Feasibility

Synora Health is technically feasible at all system layers:

  • Backend: FastAPI (Python) is a mature, high-performance framework with extensive documentation, production usage at major companies, and complete async support for handling concurrent requests.
  • Database: MongoDB is a production-grade NoSQL database suitable for healthcare data with its flexible schema design, horizontal scaling, and rich query capabilities.
  • AI: OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini provides production-ready LLM capabilities with built-in vision support. No custom model training or specialized hardware required — API is highly available with low latency.
  • Frontend: React 18 with Tailwind CSS is compatible with all modern browsers, requires no special client-side installation, and provides responsive design for desktop and mobile devices.
  • Video: Jitsi Meet provides free, open-source, browser-based video consultation without requiring native app downloads or proprietary SDKs.

# JWT Authentication Flow

# 2. Generate JWT access token (60 min)
# 3. Generate refresh token (7 days)

async def get_current_user(token: str = Depends(oauth2_scheme)):
    payload = jose.jwt.decode(token, SECRET_KEY, algorithms=["HS256"])
    user = await db.users.find_one({"_id": ObjectId(payload["sub"])})
    if not user or not user.get("is_active"):
        raise HTTPException(401, "Unauthorized")
    return serialize_doc(user)

10. Implementation

10.1 Module-wise Explanation

Module 1: Authentication Module

Role-based access control with JWT tokens. Three roles: Patient, Doctor, Admin. bcrypt (cost factor 12) password hashing. Refresh token rotation for session security. Middleware validates every protected route.

Module 2: AI Symptom Analysis Module

OpenAI GPT-4o-mini analyzes patient-described symptoms in natural language. Returns structured JSON with SHAP-style feature contributions showing which symptoms contributed most to each diagnosis. Includes confidence scores, risk levels, and specialist recommendations. Rule-based fallback for when API is unavailable.

Module 3: Report Analyzer Module

OpenAI Vision API reads medical report images (blood tests, X-rays, prescriptions). Extracts and explains every parameter with normal ranges. Color-coded status indicators. Saves results to Health Records automatically. Falls back to Google Gemini if OpenAI is unavailable.

Module 4: Appointment Booking Module

5-step flow: Type → Date/Time (timezone-safe calendar) → Details → Confirm → Payment. Mock Razorpay payment with UPI/Card/Wallet options. Auto-generates Jitsi meeting link on doctor confirmation. Sends booking confirmation email + SMS automatically.

Module 5: Video Consultation Module

Jitsi Meet embedded as iframe inside the app — no external app or plugin needed. Fullscreen toggle support. Type-specific step guides shown before entering the meeting room. Prejoin screen disabled for seamless entry.

Module 6: Health Records Module

Store prescriptions, lab reports, diagnoses as structured records. Auto-save after successful AI analysis. Doctor access to patient records during consultation. Export to PDF via browser print.

Module 7: Emergency SOS Module

One-tap SOS button with confirmation step. Captures GPS coordinates via browser geolocation API. Sends SMS to all registered emergency contacts via Twilio. Logs every SOS event with timestamp and location to MongoDB.

Module 8: Admin Analytics Module

Real-time analytics aggregated from MongoDB using aggregate pipeline. Six Recharts visualizations: weekly appointments area chart, monthly trends, consultation type distribution, revenue chart, top specializations, and AI insights panel.

Module 9: Wellness Module

Mental health assessments using validated clinical tools (PHQ-9 for depression, GAD-7 for anxiety, PSS-10 for stress). Drug interaction checker sends medicine list to OpenAI and returns interaction analysis. Family health manager stores member profiles with blood group, conditions, and allergies.

Module 10: Notifications Module

Email notifications via Gmail SMTP with HTML templates. SMS via Twilio on appointment booking, confirmation, and cancellation. WhatsApp bot via Twilio webhook — handles appointment queries and booking guidance. Graceful skip if Twilio not configured.

10.2 Working of the System

Patient Consultation Flow:

  1. Patient registers / logs in → role-based JWT token issued
  2. Describes symptoms → OpenAI GPT-4o-mini analyzes → SHAP insights returned
  3. System recommends appropriate specialist based on analysis
  4. Patient browses doctors filtered by specialization and rating
  5. Books appointment using 5-step flow with timezone-safe calendar
  6. Completes mock payment (UPI / Card / Net Banking / Wallet)
  7. Receives confirmation email + SMS
  8. Doctor receives notification → confirms → Jitsi meeting link generated
  9. Patient joins embedded video call — no external app required
  10. Doctor writes prescription → appointment marked completed → patient rates consultation

AI Report Analysis Flow:

  1. Patient uploads report image to Laboratory folder category
  2. Image converted to base64 and sent to backend
  3. Backend tries OpenAI Vision API (gpt-4o-mini) first
  4. If OpenAI fails, falls back to Google Gemini API
  5. AI extracts all parameters with values, units, and normal ranges
  6. Color-coded results shown (red = high, blue = low, green = normal)
  7. Report auto-saved to Health Records collection
  8. Doctor can access these records during future consultations

const send = async (text) => {
  const msg = (text !== undefined ? text : input).trim()
  if (!msg || loading) return
  setInput('')

  // Add user message to UI
  setMessages(prev => [...prev, { role: 'user', content: msg }])
  setLoading(true)

  try {
    // Send history WITHOUT current message (backend appends separately)
    const historyToSend = messages.slice(-8)
      .map(m => ({ role: m.role, content: m.content }))
    const res = await api.post('/ai/chat', {
      message: msg, history: historyToSend
    })
    const reply = res.data?.response || 'Could not generate response.'
    setMessages(prev => [...prev, { role: 'assistant', content: reply }])
  } catch (err) {
    const errMsg = err.response?.status === 401
      ? 'Please log in to use the AI assistant.'
      : 'AI service temporarily unavailable.'
    setMessages(prev => [...prev, { role: 'assistant', content: errMsg }])
  } finally { setLoading(false) }
}

14. Limitations

14.1 Current Constraints

  1. AI Model: Uses GPT-4o-mini which is a general-purpose language model, not a specialized medical model trained on labeled clinical datasets. Outputs should be treated as preliminary screening, not formal diagnosis.
  2. No Wearable Integration: All vital signs (heart rate, blood pressure, blood sugar) are manually entered. No real-time monitoring from smartwatches, glucometers, or IoT health devices.
  3. Limited Offline Support: The platform requires a stable internet connection for all AI features. No PWA (Progressive Web App) service worker caching implemented.
  4. No FHIR/HL7 Integration: The platform is not connected with existing hospital information systems (HIS) or Electronic Health Record (EHR) platforms via HL7 FHIR standards.
  5. Mock Payment: Payment processing is simulated. No real payment gateway (Razorpay / Stripe) integration with actual transaction processing.
  6. Voice Call: Uses Jitsi Meet with camera disabled rather than a true telephony integration (SIP / VoIP). Requires internet bandwidth for both parties.
  7. Language: Platform is English-only. The Hindi language toggle in the Navbar is a UI-only visual indicator — full translation is not yet implemented.
  8. Scalability: Not stress-tested for large concurrent user loads. Single-server deployment configuration without load balancing or CDN.
