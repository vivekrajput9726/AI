Synora Health AI Platform

CHAPTER 10 — CONCLUSION

10.1 Summary of the Project

The development of Synora Health — an AI-powered healthcare platform — has been a comprehensive and rewarding journey that involved the integration of cutting-edge technologies to address real-world challenges in the healthcare domain. This project was undertaken as part of the six-month internship at Coding House, Surat, under the M.Sc. Artificial Intelligence program at Auro University, Surat, and represents a significant contribution toward making quality healthcare accessible, intelligent, and efficient.

The platform successfully brings together three distinct user ecosystems — patients, doctors, and administrators — into a single unified system. Patients can seamlessly register, book appointments, consult AI for symptom analysis, manage their health records, and connect with doctors through real-time chat and video consultations. Doctors are empowered with AI-assisted tools for writing prescriptions, managing their schedules, viewing patient histories, and tracking earnings. Administrators are provided with a comprehensive control panel to manage users, verify doctors, monitor appointments, and analyze platform-wide statistics in real time.

The technical stack chosen for this project reflects modern industry standards. The backend, built with FastAPI (Python 3.11) and MongoDB, ensures high performance, asynchronous operations, and scalable data management. The frontend, developed with React 18, Vite, Tailwind CSS, and Redux Toolkit, delivers a responsive and intuitive user experience across desktop and mobile devices.

10.2 Achievements and Key Features Delivered

Throughout the development cycle, all planned modules were successfully implemented and tested. The following key features were delivered as part of the final platform:

  • AI Symptom Checker: Integrated with Groq Llama 3.1-70B as the primary AI engine, with Google Gemini 1.5 Pro and OpenAI GPT-4 as intelligent fallback models, providing accurate symptom analysis, urgency classification, and doctor recommendations.
  • Smart Appointment Booking: A complete end-to-end appointment flow with doctor search, slot selection, Razorpay payment integration (HMAC-SHA256 verified), and WebSocket-based real-time notifications to doctors.
  • Video Consultation: WebRTC peer-to-peer video calling with in-room text chat and screen-sharing capabilities, enabling remote healthcare consultations.
  • AI-Assisted Prescriptions: Doctors can generate AI-drafted prescriptions using Groq AI, review and edit them, and save them to patient health records with PDF export support.
  • Smart Health Records: Patients can upload medical reports, images, and documents, with Gemini Vision AI automatically analyzing and providing health insights.
  • Medicine Reminders: A fully functional medicine reminder system with dosage tracking, frequency settings, and alert functionality.
  • Emergency SOS: Real-time GPS-based emergency feature that detects nearby hospitals and alerts emergency contacts instantly.
  • JWT Authentication: Secure role-based authentication with bcrypt password hashing, Twilio SMS OTP verification, automatic token refresh, and Redux Persist for session management.
  • Admin Control Panel: A complete administration dashboard with real-time MongoDB statistics, doctor verification workflow, user management, appointment monitoring, and financial analytics.
  • Real-Time Chat: WebSocket-powered messaging system with read receipts, unread count badges, and multi-room support between patients and doctors.

10.4 Challenges Faced and Solutions Adopted

The development process encountered several technical challenges that demanded creative and effective solutions. Managing asynchronous operations between the FastAPI backend and the Motor (async MongoDB driver) required careful handling of coroutines and connection lifecycles. The integration of multiple AI APIs introduced the challenge of inconsistent response formats, which was resolved by building a unified response parser with a fallback chain.

One significant challenge was maintaining correct Redux state during page refreshes, where the loading state persisted as true after a crash, causing a stuck spinner. This was resolved by adding a persist/REHYDRATE handler in the Redux slice to reset the loading state on application restart. Additionally, implementing WebRTC for video consultations required managing peer connection signalling, ICE candidate exchange, and handling browser compatibility — all of which were addressed through careful implementation of the WebRTC API.

The doctor verification field collision — where the doctor's is_verified (admin approval) was overwriting the user's is_verified (OTP verification) during login — was identified and fixed by renaming the doctor field to doctor_verified in the serialized response. These challenges, while demanding, significantly enhanced the team's understanding of full-stack development, API integration, and real-world system design.

10.5 Limitations

While Synora Health is a comprehensive platform, there are certain limitations that exist in the current version. The AI symptom checker, while highly capable, is not a replacement for professional medical diagnosis and should be considered as a decision-support tool only. The platform currently relies on third-party AI APIs (Groq, Gemini, OpenAI), which means that API outages or rate limits could temporarily affect AI features. File storage is managed locally in the current version, and a cloud storage solution such as AWS S3 or Google Cloud Storage would be required for production deployment at scale. The video consultation feature depends on a stable internet connection for both parties, and poor network conditions may impact call quality.

10.6 Future Scope

Synora Health has been built with scalability and extensibility in mind, and there are numerous exciting directions in which the platform can be enhanced in the future:

  • Mobile Application: Development of native Android and iOS applications using React Native to provide a superior mobile experience with push notifications and offline support.
  • Wearable Device Integration: Connecting with smartwatches and health bands (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Samsung Health) to continuously monitor vital signs such as heart rate, blood pressure, and SpO2, and feed this data into the AI health analysis engine.
  • Predictive Health Analytics: Training custom ML models on anonymized patient data to predict disease risk factors, medication adherence patterns, and early warning signs of chronic conditions.
  • Multi-Language Support: Adding regional language support (Hindi, Gujarati, Tamil, etc.) to make the platform accessible to patients across rural India.
  • Insurance Integration: Direct tie-up with health insurance providers for real-time claim processing and cashless treatment facilitation.
  • Government Health Portal Integration: Integration with India's National Digital Health Mission (NDHM) / Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) for ABHA health ID linking.
  • AI Diagnostic Imaging: Advanced radiology AI using deep learning models to analyze X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans with greater precision.
  • Pharmacy Integration: Connecting with online pharmacies to allow patients to order prescribed medicines directly from the prescription module.
